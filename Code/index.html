Introduction

This project aims to present and visualize some relevant statistics, namely the gross domestic prodcut (GDP), the military spendig, the education spending ann the healthcare spending of some of the G20 countries during the 2011-2015 time range. The countries analized are the following: Australia, Brazil, Canada, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, South Korea, Mexixo, Turkey, the United Kingdom, and the United States. Initially, I wanted to include more countries and analyze a more recent time range, but the education spending dataset had many missing values, and consequently I had to pick the countries based on data availability. All the datasets this project is based on come from the World Bank Open Data Catalog with the exception of the education spending dataset which was retrieved from the website of the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD). For more information about the data, the data cleaning process, and the author of this project please visit the About section of this website. You can also find this project on Zenodo and Github.

Education and Healthcare Spending Growth

The following graphs display the growth in Education and Healthcare of the selected countries between 2011 and 2015.

By looking at the graphs above, we can clearly see that most of the countries had a negative growth for both Education and Healthcare between 2011 and 2015. This might be due to several factors, including internal cuts to Education and Healthcate budgets, reallocations of funds, and the fact that many of these countries had not fully recovered from the 2008 international crisis during the 2011-2015 time range. Among the countries in negative growth, the cases of Italy and Japan are striking. These two countries, which are considered developed countries and they are also part of the G7, had a serious negative growth in both Education and Healthcare, during the timeframe analyzed. Consequently, I decided to investigate them further to see if the negative growths can be exaplained by looking at their GDP or it is due to reasons that go beyond the scope of this project.

Zooming In: The Cases of Italy and Japan

As we can see from the graphs, the main reason for Italy and Japans' major negative growth in Education and Healthcare Spending is the dramatical decrease of their GDP. Therefore the two countries did not perform any major cut in relation to their GDP, but their spendings decreased simply because their GDP declined. This may not be the only reason why these two countries had such a negative growth in Education and Healthcate; however, it is the only one we could clearly identify with the data available.

About

Data

All the datasets utilized for this project were retrieved in June 2020 from the World Bank's Open Data Catalog, an open source, free, World Bank's Open Data Catalog that provides access to over 3,000 datasets and 14,000 indicators for over 150 countries in the world. The only dataset that was not retrived from there was the education spending dataset, which was retrieved from the OECD website.

Methodology and Data Cleaning

The datasets were downloaded in CSV format and they were cleaned and processed using both Excel and Python.The education data contained many missing values and every country that had more than one missing value per timeframe was eliminated. If there was just one missing value per timeframe, it was filled with the average of the four remaining data points. The education dataset was created by summing the expenditure for primary, secondary and tertiary education. All the charts and visualizations were created using the Google Visualization API.
